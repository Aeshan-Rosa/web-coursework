Challenges and Lessons Learned

Building my website with HTML and CSS was a rollercoaster of hurdles and breakthroughs. I struggled with messy layouts until Flexbox and Grid saved me, teaching me to adapt to modern tools. Browser quirks threw curveballs—perfect designs broke across platforms, showing me the value of relentless testing and compatibility fixes. CSS cascades turned into tangles, forcing me to learn precision with selectors and structure. Debugging tiny errors, like missing tags, made me lean on DevTools and embrace patience. Through it all, I grew—mastering responsive design, animations, and resilience. Each challenge shaped a better site and a wiser me.

Compliance

Building my website with HTML and CSS meant embracing compliance. I followed W3C standards for clean, semantic code, ensuring cross-browser reliability. Accessibility demanded alt text, ARIA tags, and keyboard-friendly design, checked with tools like WAVE. Legally, I added cookie notices, terms of use, and verified licenses for fonts and images. Compliance took effort—extra tweaks and tests—but it made my site fast, inclusive, and legit.
